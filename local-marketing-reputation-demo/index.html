Greg's Tyres & Mechanical - Mudgee

Local campaigns, without turning Greg's into a spam machine.

Quote history and completed jobs quietly create sensible campaign and review suggestions. A human approves the message, audience and send timing before anything goes out.

Operating rule Automated suggestion. Human approval.

How it works

The system watches the work Greg's already does.

No staff member needs to build a campaign by hand. The useful data is already inside quotes, jobs, vehicles, service dates and follow-up outcomes.

1

Pull the useful records

The system reads practical service signals, not broad social-media interests.

  • Quotes sent but not booked.
  • Completed tyre, battery, rego and field-service jobs.
  • Vehicle type, last visit, job notes and service timing.
2

Match the local reason

Campaigns are triggered by genuine need: 4WD holidays, farm field work, construction vehicles, rego, batteries, win-back or review requests.

  • Exclude recent opt-outs and unresolved issues.
  • Keep customers in the right service category.
  • Stop repeated messages to the same local customer.
3

Create one suggested output

The system writes the draft and explains why each customer belongs in the audience.

  • Plain SMS or email copy.
  • Optional approved retargeting list.
  • Review path only after a completed job.
4

Hold for owner approval

Mel or Nick can approve, narrow the audience, change timing, edit the wording or cancel the run.

  • No public send before approval.
  • Unhappy customers become follow-up tasks.
  • Approved sends are logged against the customer record.

What the approval card shows

Audience 4WD, ute and SUV customers with older tyre checks or all-terrain quote history.
Channels SMS first, email only where detailed quote context exists.
Review path Happy completed jobs get a review link. Unhappy replies create a staff follow-up task instead.
Risk note Keep it helpful and local. Do not over-message before holiday periods.
Data sources Quotes, jobs and service history

Greg's uses the records already produced by the quote engine, front desk and workshop.

Business control Approval before send

The system can suggest useful local contact, but owners still control audience, message and timing.

Reputation safety Recover issues before reviews

Customers with concerns are routed to a staff follow-up, not pushed straight to a public review link.

Demo data is fictional. The point is the workflow: practical local campaigns and reviews from real operational data, with approval gates before public contact.
