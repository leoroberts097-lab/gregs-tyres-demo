Greg's Tyres

Rough basic pre-audit demo for scoped work

Owner context
Owner goals

Growth needs structure, not just more sales

Greg's is already a strong local business. The map shows how the quote engine can become part of a broader operating structure.

  • About $3.5m current turnover and roughly 9 employees.
  • About $5m may be close to the workshop and space ceiling.
  • Goal is easier training, cleaner management and a business less dependent on a few key people.
Current state

Marlin, suppliers and staff knowledge all matter

Marlin remains part of the environment, but the friction is around product choice, quoting confidence, supplier complexity and knowledge sitting in people's heads.

  • Bridgestone Service Centre in Mudgee, not a franchise.
  • Front-desk tyre and fitment knowledge is concentrated in experienced staff.
  • The website is closer to a contact path than a pricing or booking path today.
Audit framing

This is a diagnostic map, not fixed build scope

The audit tests data access, workflow volume, staff adoption risk, Marlin constraints, supplier portal access, ROI and build complexity before recommending what to build first.

  • Workflow options are ranked before build decisions.
  • Online pricing keeps staff review for complex or risky jobs.
  • Supplier integration is not promised until access is validated.

Audit process: how we decide what to build first

1. Interview

Owners, front desk, technicians and admin clarify current process.

2. Observe workflows

Quote, supplier, booking and workshop handoff steps are documented.

3. Review systems

Marlin, supplier portals, website, spreadsheets and stock data are checked.

4. Score options

Workflows are ranked by ROI, effort, data access and adoption risk.

5. Roadmap

Greg's receives the recommended first build and implementation path.
